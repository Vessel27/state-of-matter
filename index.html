Solid ❄️

Shape & Volume: Definite shape and volume

Particle Arrangement: Tightly packed in a fixed, ordered structure

Particle Motion: Vibrate in place, minimal movement

Density: High

Examples: Ice, Rock, Metal, Wood, Diamond

Liquid 💧

Shape & Volume: Definite volume, takes shape of container

Particle Arrangement: Close together but not in fixed positions

Particle Motion: Can slide past each other, moderate movement

Density: Medium to High

Examples: Water, Oil, Milk, Juice, Mercury

Gas ☁️

Shape & Volume: No definite shape or volume, fills entire container

Particle Arrangement: Far apart, random distribution

Particle Motion: Move rapidly in all directions

Density: Low

Examples: Air, Oxygen, Carbon Dioxide, Helium, Steam

Melting 🔥

Process: Solid → Liquid

Energy: Heat energy is ADDED

What Happens: Particles gain energy and vibrate more vigorously, breaking free from their fixed positions. The solid structure breaks down and becomes a liquid.

Example: Ice melting into water at 0°C

Freezing ❄️

Process: Liquid → Solid

Energy: Heat energy is REMOVED

What Happens: Particles lose energy and slow down, allowing them to settle into a fixed, ordered arrangement. The liquid becomes a solid.

Example: Water freezing into ice at 0°C

Evaporation 🌡️

Process: Liquid → Gas

Energy: Heat energy is ADDED

What Happens: Particles gain enough energy to overcome intermolecular forces and escape from the liquid surface, becoming a gas.

Example: Water boiling into steam at 100°C

Condensation 💧

Process: Gas → Liquid

Energy: Heat energy is REMOVED

What Happens: Particles lose energy and slow down, allowing them to form intermolecular bonds and become a liquid.

Example: Steam condensing into water droplets

Sublimation ⬆️

Process: Solid → Gas (skips liquid phase)

Energy: Heat energy is ADDED

What Happens: Particles gain enough energy to go directly from a solid to a gas without passing through the liquid phase.

Example: Dry ice turning into CO₂ gas

Deposition ⬇️

Process: Gas → Solid (skips liquid phase)

Energy: Heat energy is REMOVED

What Happens: Particles lose energy and go directly from a gas to a solid without passing through the liquid phase.

Example: Water vapor forming frost on surfaces

Particle Behavior in Different States

❄️ Solid

Particles vibrate in fixed positions, tightly packed together with minimal movement.

💧 Liquid

Particles flow and slide past each other, maintaining close proximity while moving freely.

☁️ Gas

Particles move rapidly in all directions, spreading out to fill the entire container.
